Falak Jalali Ph.D. student, University of Iowa.

Education is perceived as a vehicle out of poverty. Literacy rate is often conflated with high levels of development and low poverty rate. Here I want to demonstrate if there is a visual relationship between literacy and poverty rate. According to the literature there exist an inverse relationship between litereacy rate the poverty rate must be less. In this project I attempt to visualise the relationship at the country level to better understand the relationship between the two variable. I conclude that there is no inverse relationship between poverty and literacy rate. The relationship is more complicated and can be dependent on other vairables like state GDP and other variables of public expenditure.
